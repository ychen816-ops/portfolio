01 — Concept

A window is not
an opening.
It is a frame

Windowscape contemplates the window not as architecture, but as a compositional device — a plane that captures light, shadow, and the passage of time without moving.

02 — Overview
Medium

Category
Visual Research

A systematic visual study of windows encountered across urban environments. Each window is treated as a specimen — collected, categorized, and recomposed to reveal unexpected resonances between form, material, and quality of light.

The project asks: what does a window know about the space it separates? What does it remember of all the light that has passed through it?

06 — Method
01 Observation
02 Collection
03 Classification
04 Recomposition

Each window was encountered and photographed in situ — nothing staged. The camera held at eye level: the most natural height of a person standing at a threshold.

The archive was then reorganized not chronologically, but by visual logic: tone, geometry, transparency, and weight. Distant windows began to speak to one another.

New adjacencies emerged. A window in one city recognized itself in another.
